Baker Commodities is one of the largest independent rendering and grease management companies in the United States, operating 21+ facilities primarily across the western U.S. with ~$311M in revenue and a workforce known for exceptional longevity — average employee tenure exceeds 20 years. Baker's core business is the collection, processing, and resale of animal byproducts and used cooking oil (UCO) through its rendering and grease divisions — a fundamentally circular-economy model that recovers 62 billion pounds of raw material annually across the U.S. and Canadian rendering industry. The renewable diesel tailwind has been transformative for the company's UCO economics: California's Low Carbon Fuel Standard and federal SAF incentives have pushed yellow grease prices to $0.48–$0.56/lb as of April 2026, up 15–22% since February, creating meaningful commodity revenue expansion at the same time Baker is investing in operational modernization. Section 03
MuleSoft Use Cases
P1 01 — CSS + ERP Integration to Salesforce: The Agentforce Data Foundation Agentforce Enablement & Service AI

Baker's COO confirmed that customer service automation — scheduling, document retrieval, invoice requests — is the top priority use case for Agentforce in FY27. The February discovery call also surfaced explicit dissatisfaction with Einstein AI's underperformance on case recommendations. Both of these problems have the same root cause: Salesforce does not have Baker's operational data. The on-premise SQL Server CSS holds the customer service history, scheduling records, and interaction logs that Agentforce needs to automate service interactions. The separate ERP holds the invoice data, billing history, and account financial records that agents must currently look up manually. Without these two systems connected to Salesforce, Agentforce is an AI engine without fuel — capable in theory, limited in practice.

MuleSoft builds a System API on Baker's SQL Server CSS that exposes customer service records, open cases, service history, and scheduling data in a normalized format. A parallel System API wraps the ERP to expose invoice history, billing status, and account financial data. A Process API assembles these sources into a unified customer account record surfaced in Salesforce — giving every Agentforce interaction and every human agent a complete, real-time view of the customer relationship. This is the API-led connectivity pattern applied to its most common and highest-value industrial use case: making an AI investment work by ensuring it has the data context it needs to resolve interactions rather than escalate them.

The business outcome is an Agentforce implementation that actually performs — automating scheduling confirmations, surfacing invoice copies without agent lookup, and providing case recommendations grounded in real service history rather than incomplete Salesforce records. Baker's COO will measure FY27 Agentforce success against specific service automation metrics; MuleSoft is what makes those metrics achievable. Without this integration, the Agentforce investment will face the same underperformance criticism that Einstein currently receives — and the renewal conversation in July 2026 will be much harder.

P1 02 — National Customer Care Center: Unified View Across 8 Divisions Service Centralization & Operations

Baker's most significant operational initiative in 2026 is the centralization of 8 divisions and 2 subsidiaries into a single National Customer Care Center by EOY. Today, each division maintains its own customer records, service scheduling, and operational data — which means a customer who operates across multiple Baker service territories has a fragmented service history spread across division-level systems. Centralizing the customer service headcount into one location does not solve this problem; it concentrates it. Agents in the National Care Center will still be unable to see a complete customer picture across all divisions without a data layer that aggregates the division-level systems into Salesforce.

MuleSoft creates an eight-division data normalization layer that pulls customer records, service history, route assignments, and scheduling data from each division's systems into a unified Salesforce account structure. Division-level data model differences — different field names, different service category codes, different scheduling formats — are resolved in the Process API layer rather than requiring any division to change its operational system. The Experience API surfaces the unified customer record to the National Care Center agents and to Agentforce Voice, which can then handle inbound calls with full account context regardless of which Baker division originally set up the customer relationship.

The business outcome is a National Care Center that opens with genuine operational intelligence rather than a consolidated headcount problem. Customer calls are resolved faster, first-call resolution rates improve, and national account managers can see their entire book of business across all divisions in Salesforce without manual consolidation. Baker's EOY 2026 centralization deadline is a hard forcing function for this integration work — the center needs to be ready when it opens, not six months after.

P1 03 — Fleet & Route Data Integration: Real-Time Service Visibility in Salesforce Field Operations & Customer Experience

Baker's service delivery model is route-based: drivers collect UCO and animal byproducts from restaurant and food service customers on scheduled routes across 21+ facility territories. When a restaurant customer calls about a missed pickup, a late collection, or a schedule change, the customer service agent needs to know where the truck is, what the current route status is, and when the customer location is next scheduled for service. Today this information almost certainly lives in the fleet GPS and route optimization system — not in Salesforce — requiring the agent to either manually call dispatch or look up a separate system, increasing handle time and reducing resolution accuracy.

MuleSoft builds a System API on Baker's fleet and route optimization platform that exposes real-time truck location, route completion status, next scheduled service dates, and driver assignment data. A Process API joins this with the customer's Salesforce account record, so that every inbound customer service call surfaces not just the customer's account history but the current operational status of their service relationship — whether their collection is on schedule, whether the route is running late, and what the next confirmed service date is. Agentforce Voice can consume this data to handle routine service status inquiries without human agent involvement.

For Baker's national account customers — restaurant chains with hundreds of locations — this capability enables proactive service communication: when a route is running behind schedule, Salesforce can trigger automated outreach to affected national account contacts before they call in. This converts a reactive customer service operation into a proactive one, which is the service quality differentiator that retains large chain accounts against regional competitor bids.

P2 04 — National Account ESG & Sustainability Reporting Automation National Accounts & ESG Compliance

Baker's circular-economy positioning — rendering that sequesters five times more greenhouse gases than it produces, equivalent to removing 12 million cars from the road — is a genuine competitive differentiator with food service chain customers navigating ESG reporting requirements under California SB 1383 and similar legislation. But translating Baker's operational performance into a customer-ready ESG report today requires manual extraction from facility processing records, route data, and volume metrics — a time-intensive process that limits Baker's ability to provide this value-add to more than its largest accounts.

MuleSoft builds a Sustainability Reporting API that aggregates per-customer collection volumes, processing outcomes, and emissions reduction calculations from Baker's facility and route data systems, normalizes them against the customer's reporting framework, and delivers an automated sustainability summary into Salesforce. National account managers can generate a customer ESG report with one click from the account record rather than manually assembling data from multiple systems. The report can be scheduled for automatic delivery ahead of each customer's reporting cycle.

The commercial outcome is a premium service deliverable that differentiates Baker from regional competitors who cannot produce credible, data-backed sustainability documentation. For food service chains with public ESG commitments — McDonald's, Starbucks, Darden Restaurants — Baker's ability to auto-generate chain-level waste diversion and emissions reduction reports becomes a retention mechanism and a competitive moat that is difficult to replicate without Baker's operational data infrastructure.

P3 05 — Commodity Price Feed to Salesforce: CFO Reporting and Account Manager Intelligence Commodity Operations & Finance

Baker's commodity business — yellow grease, inedible tallow, choice white grease — generates pricing and volume data that is currently isolated in the commodity management system and largely invisible to the Salesforce-based sales and account management function. With commodity prices up 15–22% since February 2026 driven by renewable diesel and SAF demand, the ability to surface real-time commodity price signals into Salesforce creates two distinct value streams. For CFO Robert Alves, a live commodity position view in Salesforce's analytics layer closes the reporting gap between the commodity trading system and the financial dashboard. For national account managers, commodity price context in the account record enables better-informed contract conversations with restaurant chain customers about UCO collection pricing and service fee structures.

MuleSoft creates a System API on Baker's commodity management infrastructure that exposes current market prices for each grease category, Baker's inventory positions, and recent transaction pricing. A Process API normalizes this data and routes it to two consumers: the Salesforce Analytics Cloud dashboards that serve CFO reporting, and the account record context panel that national account managers see when managing a restaurant chain relationship. The CFO gains a live commodity position view without manual export from the trading system; account managers gain pricing intelligence before customer conversations.

The outcome is a CFO reporting workflow that eliminates manual data assembly for commodity position updates and an account management function that is better equipped to handle pricing discussions with large chain customers — both of which contribute to the operational efficiency case that Robert Alves is evaluating as part of the July renewal decision.

Section 04
Solution Architecture

Baker Commodities' integration challenge is a hub-and-spoke architecture problem: a central Salesforce and Agentforce layer needs to receive real-time data from a spoke of operational systems — CSS, ERP, fleet routing, scheduling, facility operations, and commodity trading — that currently run independently across 8 divisions, 2 subsidiaries, and 21+ facilities. MuleSoft's three-layer API architecture is the correct solution because it treats each operational system as a stable backend that is wrapped rather than replaced, routes data to Salesforce through normalized Process APIs that handle the division-level data model differences, and delivers purpose-built Experience APIs to Agentforce, the National Care Center agent desktop, and the CFO analytics layer. Critically, this architecture means that as Baker's operational systems evolve — a new ERP implementation, a fleet system upgrade, a new facility acquisition — the Salesforce and Agentforce layer is shielded from those changes at the System API boundary.
