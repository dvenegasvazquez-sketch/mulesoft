Great Lakes Wines & Spirits is one of Michigan's largest independent beverage alcohol distributors, with approximately $437 million in annual revenue, 850 employees, and a route network serving 17,000 licensed retail accounts across the state. The company is governed under a Co-CEO structure shared between the Ross and Cooper families — Syd Ross and Lew Cooper III — representing a four-generation ownership partnership that has built the business through both organic growth and a significant acquisition history spanning at least nine named companies. GLWAS operates within Michigan's three-tier alcohol distribution system, which creates a regulatory environment that is materially more complex than most industries: every transaction flows through state-mandated distributor intermediaries, every SKU must be licensed at the state level, and every supplier relationship involves depletion reporting and VIP (Voluntary Incentive Program) fund management governed by both contractual and regulatory requirements. The combination of acquisition-driven growth, Michigan-specific regulatory complexity, and a 17,000-account route operation creates an integration and data infrastructure challenge that is among the most technically demanding in the mid-market distribution sector.

The technology picture at GLWAS is consistent with a company that has grown faster through acquisition than its systems have been able to consolidate. Each acquired entity — Bellino, Tom Ryan, Demorest, Oak, Gordon, Classic, Fabiano Wine, Henry Fox, and Motor City Seafood — almost certainly arrived with its own ERP, customer database, route management system, and supplier reporting infrastructure. The current Tableau Cloud deployment, confirmed active with an April 27 renewal, is a signal that GLWAS has made analytics investment — but the internal forensics review confirming product underutilization suggests that the analytics layer is only as good as the data feeding it, and that data is almost certainly fragmented across the post-acquisition system landscape. Salesforce is active in the account, and the beverage distribution vertical within Salesforce is well-established — Consumer Goods Cloud has meaningful adoption among distributors precisely because the rep routing, visit planning, order capture, promotional calendar, and category management workflows map directly onto how a distributor's sales force operates in the field. The gap between what Salesforce and Tableau could deliver for GLWAS and what they are currently delivering is an integration gap, not a capability gap.

The competitive environment for GLWAS creates additional urgency for operational modernization. Southern Glazer's — the largest wine and spirits distributor in the United States — operates at national scale with enterprise-grade data infrastructure that gives its supplier partners and retail accounts a level of analytics and reporting that independent regional distributors like GLWAS cannot match without deliberate investment. In Michigan's three-tier system, GLWAS's independence is its differentiation — local relationships, market knowledge, and supplier responsiveness that a national distributor cannot replicate. But that differentiation is only sustainable if GLWAS can compete on data quality and reporting capability with suppliers who are simultaneously served by national distributors offering sophisticated depletion analytics, promotional ROI tracking, and category management dashboards. The integration investment that connects GLWAS's ERP, route management, supplier reporting, and compliance systems to a unified Salesforce + Tableau intelligence layer is not a technology project — it is the operational infrastructure that protects the company's competitive position against Southern Glazer's in every supplier conversation GLWAS has.

Section 03
MuleSoft + Tableau Use Cases
P1 01 — Supplier Depletion & VIP Fund Reporting Automation Supplier Intelligence · MuleSoft + Tableau

GLWAS's most commercially sensitive data operation is the weekly depletion report and VIP fund reconciliation it provides to supplier partners. These reports determine how much promotional funding flows back to GLWAS and define the quality of the distributor-supplier relationship — which in the Michigan market is the primary competitive differentiator against Southern Glazer's. Today this process almost certainly involves manual extraction from multiple ERP and route management systems, manual assembly into a reporting format, and delivery through email or supplier portal upload. Every manual step introduces error risk, and every reporting inconsistency is a conversation that damages the supplier relationship GLWAS depends on.

MuleSoft builds System APIs on each ERP and route management system that expose depletion data — units sold by SKU, by account, by rep, by period — in a normalized schema. A Process API assembles the depletion picture across all source systems and calculates VIP fund accruals against the supplier contract terms. An Experience API delivers the completed depletion report to each supplier's portal in the format they require — eliminating the manual assembly step entirely. Tableau receives the same normalized depletion data stream and surfaces it in supplier-facing category management dashboards that GLWAS account managers can share in supplier reviews.

The business outcome is a supplier reporting function that runs automatically, accurately, and on time — every week, without manual intervention. GLWAS moves from a distributor that reports depletion to a distributor that provides real-time depletion intelligence, which is a qualitative leap in the supplier relationship that protects and expands VIP fund allocations. For a $437M distributor where supplier promotional funding represents a meaningful margin contribution, this use case has the clearest and most direct ROI of any integration investment available.

P1 02 — Salesforce + ERP Integration: Rep Intelligence Across 17,000 Accounts Sales Force Effectiveness · MuleSoft + Salesforce

GLWAS's sales force manages route visits and order capture across 17,000 licensed retail licensees — one of the largest retail account bases of any independent distributor in Michigan. Consumer Goods Cloud's rep routing, visit planning, and order capture capabilities are purpose-built for exactly this workflow. But those capabilities require the Salesforce account record to contain real-time order history, current inventory availability, active promotional offers, and account compliance standing — data that lives in the ERP, WMS, and compliance systems and is almost certainly not flowing into Salesforce in real time. A rep arriving at an account without this context is operating with a partial picture of the relationship at the moment that matters most.

MuleSoft connects the ERP order history, WMS inventory positions, promotional calendar data, and Michigan LCC licensee compliance status to Salesforce through a unified account enrichment Process API. Every account record in Consumer Goods Cloud becomes a complete picture of the relationship: last order date and mix, current promotional offers available, inventory of key SKUs at the warehouse, and licensee compliance standing. Route planning becomes data-driven rather than rep-intuition-driven, and order capture at the account becomes informed by real inventory and promotional availability. The same data feeds Tableau dashboards for sales management visibility into rep productivity, account coverage, and promotional execution rates.

The business outcome is a sales force that operates with the data intelligence of a national distributor while retaining the local relationship advantage of an independent. In Michigan, where Southern Glazer's competes for the same accounts with enterprise-scale data tools, this capability is a retention mechanism for GLWAS's most productive reps and a competitive argument in every supplier conversation about route effectiveness and account penetration.

P3 05 — Michigan LCC Compliance Automation Regulatory Compliance · MuleSoft

Michigan's three-tier alcohol distribution system requires GLWAS to maintain precise licensee records, transaction histories, and compliance status tracking for every one of its 17,000 retail accounts. The Michigan Liquor Control Commission's reporting requirements — license verification before delivery, transaction reporting, and periodic compliance submissions — are a regulatory overhead that scales with account volume and is currently managed through processes that are almost certainly manual-intensive given the post-acquisition system fragmentation. A compliance failure or reporting error in this environment carries real regulatory consequences.

MuleSoft builds a Compliance Data API that pulls real-time licensee status from the Michigan LCC's data systems, matches it against GLWAS's active account list in Salesforce, and flags compliance exceptions before a delivery is dispatched. The same API layer feeds automated compliance reporting to the LCC on the required schedule, eliminating the manual extraction and submission process. Salesforce account records display current licensee compliance status for every retail account — giving reps and customer service teams immediate visibility into whether an account can legally receive a delivery before the truck is loaded.

For a distributor whose entire business depends on operating within the three-tier system's regulatory framework, compliance automation is both a risk management investment and an operational efficiency gain — and it is a capability that GLWAS's supplier partners will value as evidence of the distributor's operational sophistication.

Solution Architecture

GLWAS's architecture challenge is a classic hub-and-spoke integration problem: multiple operational backends — ERPs, fleet and route systems, WMS, compliance systems, EDI feeds — need to deliver consistent, real-time data to a central intelligence layer composed of Salesforce (engagement and CRM), Tableau (analytics and supplier reporting), and a compliance reporting function. MuleSoft's three-layer API architecture solves this without requiring any backend system to be replaced — each operational system is wrapped in a System API, cross-system business logic runs in Process APIs, and Salesforce and Tableau receive clean, governed data through Experience APIs. The architecture absorbs future acquisitions at the System API layer, meaning each new company GLWAS acquires adds one integration rather than N custom connections.
